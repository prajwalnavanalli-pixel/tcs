About Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is one of the most significant and sacred temples in India, dedicated to Lord Ram. Built at the believed birthplace of Lord Ram, this grand temple is a symbol of faith, cultural pride, and national heritage.

Designed in traditional Nagara style, the temple showcases intricate carvings, beautiful architecture, and divine atmosphere that draws millions of devotees and tourists every year.

Temple History

The site of Ram Janmabhoomi has been a center of faith and devotion for centuries. The construction of the Ram Mandir followed a landmark Supreme Court judgment in 2019, resolving a decades-long legal dispute.

The Bhoomi Pujan (ground-breaking ceremony) was performed on 5th August 2020 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi, and since then, construction has continued with blessings and contributions from devotees worldwide.
